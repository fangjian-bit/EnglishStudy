# 09 · 长段落对照

> 较长语篇：每段先英文后中文，适合对照阅读与复述练习。  
> 返回 → [索引.md](./索引.md)

### 1

**EN:**
When I first arrived in a foreign country, everything sounded fast and unfamiliar. I smiled at the airport officer and understood only half of what he said. Instead of feeling defeated, I wrote down the phrases I missed and looked them up that night. The next morning, I ordered coffee using a full sentence, and the barista answered slowly and kindly. That small success gave me confidence for the rest of the week.

**CN:**
刚到国外时，一切都听起来又快又陌生。我对机场工作人员微笑，却只听懂了他说的一半。我没有气馁，把没听懂的短语记下来，当晚就去查。第二天早上，我用完整句子点了咖啡，咖啡师放慢语速、友善地回应。这点小成功让我一整周都有了信心。

### 2

**EN:**
My hometown is not famous, but it is the place where I learned to ride a bike and to say goodbye. In summer, the river behind our school turned green with algae, and boys jumped in despite their mothers' warnings. In winter, the same river froze thin enough to crack under a careless foot. Years later, when I visit, the streets look smaller, yet the smell of fried dough at the market corner is exactly the same.

**CN:**
我的家乡并不出名，却是我学会骑车、学会道别的地方。夏天，学校后面的河水泛绿，男孩们不顾妈妈的警告往下跳。冬天，同一条河结冰薄得经不起一脚踩错。多年后回访，街道显得更窄了，但街角早点摊油炸面食的气味一模一样。

---

### 3

**EN:**
Learning a language is less like memorizing a dictionary and more like training a muscle. You cannot read about running and expect to win a race; you have to move your legs again and again until the motion feels natural. Grammar rules help, but they matter most when you are already trying to speak. The learners who improve fastest are not always the smartest—they are the ones who show up every day, even when progress feels invisible.

**CN:**
学语言不像背词典，更像练肌肉。不能只读跑步知识就指望赢得比赛；得反复动腿，直到动作自然。语法有帮助，但最重要的是你已经在开口说的时候。进步最快的不一定最聪明，而是每天都出现的人，哪怕进步一时看不见。

### 4

**EN:**
The office was quiet after five, except for the hum of the air conditioner and the occasional ping of a late email. I stayed to finish a report that my manager needed for Monday's meeting. Outside the window, the city lights came on one block at a time, as if someone were switching on a giant circuit. I saved the file, backed it up twice, and finally allowed myself to think about dinner.

**CN:**
五点以后办公室很安静，只有空调嗡嗡声和偶尔迟到的邮件提示音。我留下来完成经理周一会议要用的报告。窗外，城市灯光一块街区一块街区地亮起，像有人在打开巨大的电路。我保存文件，备份了两遍，这才允许自己去想晚饭。

### 5

**EN:**
Travel teaches you that maps are only a starting point. We planned a strict schedule for our trip to the coast, but a delayed train changed everything. We missed one museum and found a night market we had never heard of. The food was cheap, the music was loud, and strangers showed us how to eat grilled squid without burning our fingers. By the end of the night, we agreed that accidents sometimes make the best memories.

**CN:**
旅行让你明白，地图只是起点。我们给海岸之旅定了严格行程，一班延误的火车改变了一切。我们错过了一座博物馆，却找到一个听都没听过的夜市。食物便宜，音乐很响，陌生人教我们如何吃烤鱿鱼而不烫到手。夜深时我们一致认为，意外有时造就最好的回忆。

### 6

**EN:**
Health is easy to ignore when you are young and busy. I used to skip breakfast, drink too much coffee, and treat sleep as something I could borrow from the weekend. Then a routine checkup showed that my habits were catching up with me. The doctor did not scold me; she simply explained what each number on the report meant. I left the clinic with a short list of changes that felt difficult but possible.

**CN:**
年轻忙碌时，健康最容易被忽视。我曾不吃早餐、咖啡喝太多，把睡眠当成可以从周末借来的东西。一次常规体检显示，习惯正在追上我。医生没有责备，只解释报告上每个数字的含义。我离开诊所时带着一份简短的改变清单，难，但做得到。

### 7

**EN:**
People often argue about technology as if it were either a hero or a villain. In reality, it is a tool whose value depends on how we use it. My phone helps me study languages on the bus, but it also steals minutes I meant to spend reading. I do not think the solution is to throw devices away. The harder, more useful task is to decide what we want our attention to serve.

**CN:**
人们谈论科技，常把它当成英雄或反派。实际上它是工具，价值取决于我们怎么用。手机帮我在公交上学语言，也会偷走我本打算用来阅读的时间。我认为解决办法不是扔掉设备。更难也更有用的是：决定我们希望注意力为谁服务。

### 8

**EN:**
The first day at a new school is a test of courage disguised as paperwork. I filled out forms, chose a locker, and walked hallways where laughter echoed from rooms I did not know. At lunch, I sat alone until another new student asked whether the seat was free. We discovered we lived on the same street and had both forgotten our gym clothes. Friendship, I learned, often begins with a shared mistake.

**CN:**
在新学校的第一天，是一场伪装成填表手续的勇气考验。我填表、选储物柜，走在走廊上，不知名教室里传来笑声。午饭时我独自坐着，直到另一个新生问位子是否空着。我们发现住同一条街，都忘了带体育课衣服。我明白了，友谊常从共同的糗事开始。

### 9

**EN:**
Climate change can feel abstract until you see a place you love change within a decade. The glacier our guide pointed to had retreated far up the mountain since his father's time. The village below now worries about floods in spring and water shortages in late summer. Scientists, farmers, and local officials meet in the same hall, not because they agree on everything, but because the problem is larger than any single job title.

**CN:**
气候变化可以很抽象，直到你看见心爱的地方十年间变了样。向导指的冰川相比他父亲那一代已退到山上很远。山下村庄现在担心春汛和夏末缺水。科学家、农民和地方官员在同一厅堂会面，不是因为事事一致，而是因为问题比任何职务都大。

### 10

**EN:**
A good conversation is not a debate you must win. It is a space where two minds test ideas without treating disagreement as insult. When my colleague challenged my proposal, I felt my face grow warm, but I asked her to explain her concerns in detail. She wanted slower rollout, clearer training, and a backup plan if the software failed. We revised the timeline together, and the final version was stronger than either of us had imagined alone.

**CN:**
好的对话不是必须赢的辩论，而是两个头脑检验想法、不把分歧当侮辱的空间。同事质疑我的方案时，我脸发热，但请她详细说明顾虑。她希望上线更慢、培训更清楚，并在软件故障时有备用方案。我们一起改了时间表，最终版本比各自单独想的都更扎实。

### 11

**EN:**
Cooking connects generations in ways that recipes alone cannot explain. My mother never measured salt; she tasted the soup and adjusted until it felt right. I copied her movements as a child, clumsy and impatient, spilling water on the counter. Now when I make the same soup in my own kitchen, I understand that she was teaching me attention, not just technique. The flavor brings her voice back into the room.

**CN:**
做饭以食谱无法单独说明的方式连接几代人。母亲从不量盐；她尝汤，调到觉得对了为止。小时候我笨拙急躁地模仿她，把水洒到台面上。如今在自己厨房做同一碗汤，我才明白她教的是用心，不只是手法。那味道让她的声音又回到房间里。

### 12

**EN:**
Public libraries remain one of the quiet wonders of modern cities. Anyone can enter without buying anything, sit for hours, and borrow worlds bound in paper or light on a screen. During exam season, students occupy every table, whispering formulas and dates like shared passwords. Elderly readers linger in the magazine section, tracing headlines with careful fingers. A library is not only a building; it is a promise that knowledge should be reachable.

**CN:**
公共图书馆仍是现代城市安静的奇迹之一。任何人无需购买即可进入，坐上几小时，借走纸上的世界或屏幕上的光。考试季里，学生占满每张桌子，低声念着公式和日期，像共享密码。年长读者在杂志区徘徊，用手指小心划过标题。图书馆不只是一栋楼，更是知识应当触手可及的一份承诺。

### 13

**EN:**
Failure hurts most when you believed success was guaranteed. I prepared for the interview for weeks, rehearsed answers, and still received a polite rejection email on Friday afternoon. For two days I replayed every sentence I had said, searching for a single fatal mistake. Then a friend reminded me that hiring depends on fit as much as skill. I applied again elsewhere, changed a few stories, and eventually landed a role that suited me better.

**CN:**
当你以为成功稳了时，失败最痛。我为面试准备数周，演练回答，仍在周五下午收到礼貌的拒信。两天里我回放自己说的每句话，寻找致命错误。朋友提醒我，录用既看能力也看契合。我在别处再申请，换了几个故事，最终得到更适合自己的职位。

### 14

**EN:**
Cities at night reveal a second personality. Daytime traffic gives way to neon signs, late diners, and cleaners sweeping sidewalks before dawn. I used to fear walking home after midnight, clutching my bag and avoiding eye contact. Over time, I learned which streets stayed busy and which shortcuts were unsafe. Confidence did not come from ignoring risk, but from observing patterns and making calmer choices.

**CN:**
夜晚的城市露出第二副面孔。白日车流让位给霓虹、深夜食客和黎明前扫街的人。我曾害怕午夜走回家，紧抓包袋、避免眼神接触。久而久之，我摸清哪些街仍热闹、哪些近路不安全。信心不是来自忽视风险，而是观察规律、做出更冷静的选择。

### 15

**EN:**
If you are learning English, measure progress in months, not days. There will be weeks when new words slip away the moment you close the notebook, and days when a stranger understands your joke without asking you to repeat it. Keep a simple record: books finished, conversations attempted, mistakes corrected. Language learning is a long road, but every step you do not abandon becomes part of who you are.

**CN:**
若你在学英语，请以月而非以日衡量进步。会有几周，新词一合笔记本就溜走；也会有陌生人听懂你的玩笑、不必请你重复的日子。做简单记录：读完的书、尝试的对话、改正的错误。学语言是长路，但你不放弃的每一步，都会成为你的一部分。
