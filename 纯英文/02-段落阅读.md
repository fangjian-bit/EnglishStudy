# 02 · 段落阅读

> 短文段落，每段 3–5 句。

## 1 · Morning Routine

Every weekday, Maya wakes up at six-thirty and drinks a glass of water before anything else. She spends fifteen minutes stretching and then makes a simple breakfast—usually eggs, toast, and fruit. On the way to work, she listens to an English podcast about science and culture. She says this quiet hour helps her feel calm before a busy day.

---

## 2 · A City Park

Central Park sits in the middle of the city, surrounded by tall office buildings. In the morning, joggers circle the paths while elderly people practice tai chi under the trees. On weekends, families spread blankets on the grass and children fly kites near the pond. Even in winter, a few visitors come to walk and watch the birds.

---

## 3 · Learning from Mistakes

When Tom first moved abroad, he was afraid to speak English in shops and restaurants. He worried that people would laugh at his pronunciation. One day, a cashier smiled and said, "Your English is fine—keep trying." That short sentence changed his attitude. He began to speak more, and his confidence grew week by week.

---

## 4 · Healthy Eating

Nutrition experts often say that no single food is magic. A healthy plate usually includes vegetables, lean protein, and whole grains. Processed snacks are convenient, but they are often high in sugar and salt. Cooking simple meals at home gives you more control over what you eat. Over time, small changes in diet can improve energy and sleep.

---

## 5 · Working from Home

Remote work saves commuting time, but it also blurs the line between office and home. Without a clear schedule, some people work late into the night. Others struggle with loneliness because they miss casual chats with colleagues. Setting fixed work hours and taking regular breaks can help maintain balance. A short walk after lunch often makes the afternoon more productive.

---

## 6 · The Public Library

The public library is more than a place to borrow books. It offers free Wi-Fi, quiet study rooms, and community events for all ages. Students use it to prepare for exams, while job seekers attend resume workshops. Librarians can help you find reliable sources for research projects. Best of all, a library card costs nothing for local residents.

---

## 7 · Climate and Daily Choices

Climate change is a global issue, but daily choices still matter. Taking public transport, reducing waste, and saving energy at home all contribute in small ways. Some people plant trees or join neighborhood clean-up days. Governments and companies must make larger changes, yet individual habits set an example for others. Progress often begins with one repeatable action.

---

## 8 · A Short Trip to the Coast

Last spring, we drove three hours to a small town on the coast. The air smelled of salt, and seagulls cried above the harbor. We ate fish and chips at a wooden table near the water. In the evening, the sky turned pink, and the waves sounded steady against the shore. We returned home tired but refreshed, already planning the next visit.

---

## 9 · Preparing for an Interview

Before an interview, it helps to research the company and review the job description. Prepare two or three examples that show your skills and teamwork. Dress neatly, arrive early, and bring copies of your resume. During the conversation, listen carefully and answer with clear, honest examples. Afterward, send a brief thank-you email to express your interest.

---

## 10 · Weekend Market

Every Saturday morning, vendors set up stalls along the old town square. Farmers sell tomatoes, herbs, and honey from nearby villages. Craft makers display handmade soap, woven baskets, and small pottery bowls. Musicians play acoustic songs near the fountain while children chase pigeons between the tables. By noon, the square smells of coffee, grilled corn, and fresh bread. Shoppers leave with full bags and plans for lunch at home.

---

## 11 · Learning a New Skill Online

When Nina decided to learn basic photography, she did not buy an expensive camera right away. She borrowed one from a friend and watched free tutorials on composition and lighting. Each week she took fifty photos and deleted most of them after review. She asked for feedback in an online forum and slowly improved her eye for detail. After three months, her landscape shots looked sharp enough to print and frame.

---

## 12 · Neighborhood Recycling

The city introduced color-coded bins for paper, plastic, glass, and general waste. At first, many residents confused which items belonged where. Volunteers visited apartment buildings and posted simple charts in the lobby. Within a few weeks, contamination in the recycling stream dropped noticeably. The program also reduced landfill costs, which the council reinvested in park maintenance. Small labels on bins turned a confusing chore into a daily habit.

---

## 13 · A Quiet Evening at Home

After a hectic week, Leo turned off his work notifications and cooked pasta from scratch. He opened the window to let in cool air and played a soft jazz playlist. His roommate joined him at the table, and they talked about nothing urgent for once. No phones sat between them, only two plates and a shared salad. When the dishes were done, Leo felt rested rather than bored. Sometimes the best plan is simply to slow down.

---

## 14 · Visiting a Science Museum

The science museum opened a new exhibit about oceans and climate. Interactive screens showed how currents move heat around the planet. Children pressed buttons to simulate rising sea levels on a digital map. A guide explained that coral reefs support fish populations humans rely on for food. Adults read panels about renewable energy and left with pamphlets for school projects. Museums turn abstract news headlines into experiences you can touch and question.

---

## 15 · Saving for a Goal

Priya wanted to visit her cousin in another country within a year. She listed her monthly expenses and found small cuts: fewer delivery meals, one fewer streaming service. She moved a fixed amount into a savings account the day after each paycheck. When unexpected costs appeared, she adjusted other categories instead of stopping entirely. Tracking progress on a simple chart kept her motivated through dull weeks. By spring, she had enough for the ticket and a short stay abroad.
