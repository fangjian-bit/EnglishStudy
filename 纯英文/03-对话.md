## 1 · At a Coffee Shop

**Barista:** Hi there. What can I get for you today?

**Customer:** I'd like a medium latte, please. Can I have oat milk instead of regular milk?

**Barista:** Sure. Would you like that hot or iced?

**Customer:** Hot, please. And do you have any pastries?

**Barista:** We have croissants and blueberry muffins fresh from the oven.

**Customer:** I'll take a croissant as well.

**Barista:** That'll be eight fifty. Pay at the counter when you're ready.

**Customer:** Here you go. Thanks!

**Barista:** Your name?

**Customer:** Alex.

**Barista:** Great. I'll call you when it's ready, Alex.

## 2 · Asking for Directions

**Tourist:** Excuse me. Could you tell me how to get to the history museum?

**Local:** Of course. Go straight down this street for about five minutes.

**Tourist:** OK, straight ahead.

**Local:** You'll pass a bank on your left. At the traffic light, turn right onto Oak Avenue.

**Tourist:** Turn right at the light. Got it.

**Local:** The museum is on the left, next to the park. You can't miss the big stone entrance.

**Tourist:** Is it far to walk?

**Local:** Not really—maybe ten minutes from here. There's also a bus, but walking is fine.

**Tourist:** Thank you so much for your help.

**Local:** You're welcome. Enjoy your visit!

## 3 · At the Doctor's Office

**Receptionist:** Good morning. Do you have an appointment?

**Patient:** Yes. My name is Chen Li. Ten o'clock with Dr. Brown.

**Receptionist:** Please fill out this form and have a seat. The doctor will see you shortly.

**Patient:** Thank you.

*(A few minutes later)*

**Doctor:** Hello, Mr. Chen. What brings you in today?

**Patient:** I've had a sore throat and a slight fever since yesterday.

**Doctor:** Any cough or trouble breathing?

**Patient:** A little cough, but breathing is fine.

**Doctor:** Let me take a look. Open your mouth, please… It's likely a mild infection. Rest, drink fluids, and take this medicine twice a day.

**Patient:** How long should I stay home from work?

**Doctor:** If the fever is gone in two days, you can return. Call us if it gets worse.

**Patient:** I will. Thank you, Doctor.

## 4 · Shopping for Clothes

**Shop Assistant:** Can I help you find something?

**Customer:** Yes. I'm looking for a jacket for spring—something light but warm.

**Shop Assistant:** We have these windbreakers and these cotton blazers over here.

**Customer:** I like the navy one. Do you have it in medium?

**Shop Assistant:** Let me check… Yes, we do. Would you like to try it on?

**Customer:** Please. Where are the fitting rooms?

**Shop Assistant:** Just around the corner on the right.

*(After trying)*

**Customer:** It fits well. How much is it?

**Shop Assistant:** It's sixty-five dollars, but there's a ten percent discount today.

**Customer:** Great. I'll take it.

**Shop Assistant:** Would you like a receipt sent to your email?

**Customer:** Yes, please.

## 5 · Making Plans with a Friend

**Sam:** Hey, are you free this Saturday?

**Jordan:** I think so. What's up?

**Sam:** A new art exhibition opened downtown. Want to go?

**Jordan:** Sounds fun. What time?

**Sam:** How about two in the afternoon? We could grab coffee afterward.

**Jordan:** Two works for me. Should we meet at the gallery?

**Sam:** Yes. I'll send you the address. Oh, tickets are fifteen dollars online.

**Jordan:** I'll buy mine tonight. Let me know if the plan changes.

**Sam:** Will do. Looking forward to it!

**Jordan:** Me too. See you Saturday!

## 6 · A Job Interview (Opening)

**Interviewer:** Thanks for coming in today. Please have a seat.

**Candidate:** Thank you for the opportunity.

**Interviewer:** To start, could you tell me a little about yourself and your background?

**Candidate:** Sure. I studied marketing and have three years of experience in social media and content planning. In my last role, I managed campaigns for a small tech company and increased engagement by about thirty percent.

**Interviewer:** What attracted you to this position?

**Candidate:** I admire your company's focus on education products. I want to work on projects that help people learn, and I think my skills fit your team well.

**Interviewer:** Can you describe a challenge you faced at work and how you handled it?

**Candidate:** Last year we launched a campaign with a tight deadline. Two team members were sick, so I reorganized tasks, set clear daily goals, and we delivered on time. I learned to communicate early when resources are limited.

**Interviewer:** Thank you. We'll discuss salary and next steps toward the end. Do you have any questions for us?

**Candidate:** Yes. What does success look like in the first six months for this role?

**Interviewer:** Good question. We'd expect you to own two major campaigns and build strong relationships with our partner schools…

## 7 · At the Pharmacy

**Customer:** Excuse me. I need something for a headache and a sore throat.

**Pharmacist:** Have you had these symptoms long?

**Customer:** Since last night. No fever, but my throat feels dry.

**Pharmacist:** Any allergies to medication?

**Customer:** I'm allergic to penicillin.

**Pharmacist:** Thanks for telling me. I'd suggest this pain reliever and a throat lozenge pack. Drink plenty of water.

**Customer:** Should I see a doctor if it doesn't improve?

**Pharmacist:** If it lasts more than three days or you get a high fever, yes. Otherwise, rest and follow the label.

**Customer:** How often can I take the tablets?

**Pharmacist:** Every six hours, with food if your stomach is sensitive. Don't exceed four in twenty-four hours.

**Customer:** Got it. I'll take these.

**Pharmacist:** Feel better soon.

## 8 · Calling Customer Service

**Agent:** Thank you for calling TechHelp. My name is Rita. How can I assist you?

**Caller:** Hi. My internet stopped working about an hour ago. I already restarted the router.

**Agent:** I can help with that. Can I confirm the account phone number on file?

**Caller:** Sure. It's 555-0142.

**Agent:** Thank you. I see a brief outage in your area that should clear within thirty minutes.

**Caller:** So I don't need a technician to come out?

**Agent:** Not unless it's still down after the outage ends. Try unplugging the router for one full minute, then plug it back in.

**Caller:** OK, I'll do that. Will I get a credit on my bill?

**Agent:** Yes. I'll apply a one-day credit automatically. You'll see it on your next statement.

**Caller:** Perfect. Thanks for explaining.

**Agent:** You're welcome. Is there anything else I can help with today?

**Caller:** That's all. Goodbye.

## 9 · Renting a Car

**Clerk:** Welcome to CityDrive. Do you have a reservation?

**Traveler:** Yes. Under the name Martin Lee. Pickup today, return Friday.

**Clerk:** Compact sedan, automatic. I'll need your driver's license and a credit card.

**Traveler:** Here you go. Is insurance included?

**Clerk:** Basic liability is included. Collision coverage is optional—fifteen dollars per day.

**Traveler:** I'll add collision, just in case. What about fuel?

**Clerk:** Return it with a full tank, or we charge a refueling fee. The car takes regular unleaded.

**Traveler:** Where do I drop it off on Friday?

**Clerk:** Any branch in the city before six p.m. I'll email your contract and the gate exit code.

**Traveler:** Great. Where are the keys?

**Clerk:** Spot 12 in the garage. The silver sedan with plate CD-4471.

## 10 · Discussing a Group Project

**Emma:** We should divide the presentation before the weekend. There are four sections.

**Noah:** I'll take the introduction and background research.

**Emma:** I'll handle the case study and the charts.

**Noah:** What about the conclusion and Q&A prep?

**Emma:** Let's ask Lin. She said she prefers speaking parts.

**Noah:** Good idea. We also need a shared folder for sources.

**Emma:** I created one on the drive. I'll drop the outline there tonight.

**Noah:** Can we meet Tuesday to rehearse? Class is Thursday.

**Emma:** Tuesday at four in the library works for me.

**Noah:** Same here. I'll message Lin and confirm everyone's slides by Wednesday.

**Emma:** Perfect. Let's keep each section under five minutes.

**Noah:** Agreed. Shorter is better for the audience.
